Fitness & Recreation



Recreation Programs

Intramurals

Intramurals are a great way to get out and play a sport you enjoy and an opportunity to meet new friends. Intramurals are for everyone: students, staff, faculty and alumni. Lead your team to the championship game and have a great time in the process!

Sport Clubs

Those who want to be a part of a competitive league have several options with the student-led Spartan Sport Clubs. As a competitive program, all teams participate in club leagues at a local level to represent Trinity Western University as Spartans.

Special Events

Every semester, TWU Recreation puts on a number of special events and tournaments, so if you love to be active and get competitive, we have something for you! Our events are a great way to meet new people, have fun, and make memories that will last a lifetime.

Fitness

Yoga and spin classes for students, staff, faculty, and alumni. Classes are offered in groups and small groups with lesson packs and drop-in prices available.

Well-Being Programs

This free program is meant to assist those wanting to improve themselves in various areas of well-being, including sleep, nutrition, physical activity, spiritual health, and more.

Personal Training

Get fit with TWU's own certified personal trainers. Our student trainers are experienced and professional—ready to help you reach your fitness goals either in person or online.

Student Business Clubs

The School of Business offers numerous student-run business clubs, including the following:

Student Business Association (SBA)

The Student Business Association (SBA) conducts social and educational events throughout the year. It's always looking for ways to add value to your experience as a business student. It helps you and your peers connect—not just with the course material but also with professors, with one another, and with the external marketplace.

Trinity Accounting Association (TAA)

The Trinity Accounting Association (TAA) is a group of Trinity Western University accounting students seeking networking opportunities and supporting one another as they prepare for the CPA designation. Their goal is to provide relevant events to engage accounting students and provide current information to other students studying accounting and considering entering the profession. The TAA also supports students who are trying to figure out whether accounting may fit into their career path.

Student Managed Investment Fund (SMIF)

The TWU Student Managed Investment Fund (SMIF) aims to provide you with hands-on experience in equity valuation and investment by using endowed funds to invest in the stock and bond markets. Since the School of Business is always striving to make a difference beyond the walls of TWU, profits earned within the fund will be allocated to specified charities and projects on an annual basis.

Young Women In Leadership (YWIL)

The Young Women in Leadership (YWIL) is a group of young women at TWU who are nurturing a community of women leaders, business professionals, and entrepreneurs. Led by women from multiple programs at TWU, YWIL seeks to encourage and empower women to embrace their strengths, push through challenges, and grow as leaders. The organization supports women by providing essential career resources, networking opportunities, and workshops to its members, as well as annual public conferences open to the business community.

Human Resources Student Association (HRSA)

The Human Resources Student Association (HRSA) is a student-run club that strives to connect students, alumni, and HR experts. HRSA seeks to explore opportunities available with a CPHR certification and/or membership.
